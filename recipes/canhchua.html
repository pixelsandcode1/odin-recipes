What is Canh Chua?

This soul warming soup was first intorduced to me 9 years ago by my then boyfriend, and current husband. He told me this sweet and sour soup was a dish of comort when growing up and it quickly became one of my favorite dishes to prepare. In hot or cold weather, the savory herb filled soup is a delight to taste and to smell. Each bite, especially when made with salmon, my fish of choice, can be complimented with a salty, garlicky, and spicy dipping sauce known as nuoc cham. Don't skip out on it!

How do you make Canh Chua?

To make canh chua:

  1. Blend the pineapple and cut up all veggies.
  2. Descale fish and cut it into fillets.
  3. Add 8 cups of water, the pineapple, and fish sauce to the pot.
  4. Pre-boil, add the onion and tomato.
  5. Once the pot is boiling, add the rest of the veggies.
  6. Simmer for 10 minutes and then add the herbs in the last minute.
  7. Let it cool down to a comfortable temperature and enjoy!

To make nuoc cham:

  1. In a jar, mix together: 3 tbsp fish sauce, 3 tbsp sugar, 9 tbsp water, 1/2 tsp lime juice, 1/2 tsp vinegar, and 4 cloves of minced garlic.
  2. If you would like to make it spicy, add 1 diced thai chili to start and additional for more heat.

To make the side of rice:

  1. Use a rice cooker on the plain setting. OR Cook on the stove with usual rice cooking steps.
  2. To make the rice more nutrious, add 1/2 cup of quinoa or another fibrous grain.
